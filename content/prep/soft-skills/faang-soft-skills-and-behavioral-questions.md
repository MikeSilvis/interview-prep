# FAANG+ Soft Skills & Behavioral Interview Questions — Template

This file is a **shared question and rubric template** for non-coding interviews at large tech companies (Meta, Google, Amazon, Apple, Netflix, etc.).

- **Use this as an interviewer/study guide.**
- Do **not** add your own personal stories here.
- Put your own answers in `users/<your-name>/soft-skills/` or similar.

## Meta (Facebook) — Product Sense, Execution, Leadership

Meta’s non-coding interviews typically focus on **three pillars**: Product Sense, Execution, and Leadership.

**What interviewers are actively looking for:**
- Ability to **identify real user problems** and articulate a clear product vision.
- Comfort with **defining and using metrics** to guide decisions.
- Evidence that you can **navigate ambiguity**, make trade-offs, and still ship.
- Clear examples of **influencing others and leading without authority**.

### Product Sense

**Example prompts:**
- Design a **new product** or improve an existing one for a specific audience (e.g. “How would you improve Facebook Groups for event organizers?”).
- Pick a Meta product you like and **walk through a new feature from idea → launch**.
- Tell me about a time you used **data or user feedback** to change a product direction.

**Depth prompts:**
- Who is the **primary user** and what problem are we solving?
- How do you define **success metrics** for this idea?
- What trade-offs are you making between **simplicity vs. power**, or **short term vs. long term**?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Product thinking, customer empathy, prioritization, metrics.

### Execution & Leadership

**Example prompts:**
- Tell me about a time you **drove a project from ambiguous requirements to launch**.
- Describe a situation where you had to **push back** on a product/manager request.
- Tell me about a time you **aligned multiple teams** around a decision.

**Depth prompts:**
- How did you structure the work and **make trade-offs**?
- How did you handle **disagreement or misalignment**?
- What **metrics** or evidence did you use to decide whether to ship/rollback?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Ownership, collaboration, decision-making, impact.

## Google — GCA, Googleyness, Leadership

Google focuses on **General Cognitive Ability (GCA)**, **Googleyness** (values & collaboration), and **Leadership**.

**What interviewers are actively looking for:**
- **Structured, logical thinking** when approaching new or messy problems.
- Signs of **curiosity and adaptability** — you enjoy learning and can ramp quickly.
- Strong **collaboration and humility**, especially in how you handle disagreement.
- A track record of **supporting others** and creating a healthy team environment.

### General Cognitive Ability (GCA)

**Example prompts:**
- Walk me through how you’d **break down a complex, unfamiliar problem**.
- Talk about a time you had to **learn something quickly** to unblock a project.
- Given an ambiguous situation, how would you **form a plan with limited data**?

**Depth prompts:**
- What options did you generate, and how did you **compare them**?
- How did you test if your approach was **working**?
- In hindsight, what would you **do differently**?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Structured thinking, curiosity, adaptability.

### Googleyness & Leadership

**Example prompts:**
- Tell me about a time you **helped a teammate succeed**.
- Describe a time you had a strong opinion but **changed your mind**.
- When have you **created an inclusive environment** or supported someone who felt excluded?

**Depth prompts:**
- How did you handle **conflict or disagreement**?
- How did you make sure everyone was **heard**?
- What did you **learn about yourself** from this situation?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Collaboration, humility, respect, psychological safety.

## Amazon — Leadership Principles

Amazon interviews are heavily anchored in their **Leadership Principles** (LPs). Expect multiple questions per LP.

**What interviewers are actively looking for:**
- Clear stories where you **owned outcomes end-to-end** (not just tasks).
- Evidence of **bias for action** paired with good judgment (not recklessness).
- Willingness to **dive deep into details** when it matters.
- Concrete examples of **earning and repairing trust** with colleagues and stakeholders.

### Ownership, Bias for Action, Deliver Results

**Example prompts:**
- Tell me about a time you **owned a problem end-to-end**.
- Describe a situation where you had to **move fast with imperfect information**.
- Talk about a goal you **initially missed**. What happened and what did you do?

**Depth prompts:**
- How did you **decide what to prioritize**?
- What specific actions did you take to **recover or course-correct**?
- How did you measure whether you **actually delivered**?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Ownership, urgency, accountability, resilience.

### Dive Deep, Learn and Be Curious, Earn Trust

**Example prompts:**
- Describe a time you **dug into data or details** to uncover the real issue.
- Tell me about something you **taught yourself** that meaningfully changed your work.
- Describe a time you **lost someone’s trust**. What happened next?

**Depth prompts:**
- What signals told you that the **surface explanation was wrong**?
- How did you communicate your findings and **influence others**?
- What did you change to **rebuild or maintain trust**?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Analytical rigor, learning mindset, trust-building.

## Apple — Craft, Collaboration, Product Quality

Apple tends to emphasize **craft, polish, cross-functional collaboration, and end-to-end ownership**.

**What interviewers are actively looking for:**
- A high **bar for quality and user experience**, with examples of sweating details.
- Ability to **balance design, technical, and product constraints** without losing coherence.
- Strong **cross-functional collaboration** with design/PM/other platforms.
- Ownership mindset around **long-term maintainability** and product integrity.

### Craft & Quality

**Example prompts:**
- Tell me about a product or feature where you **sweated the details**.
- Describe a time you **pushed back on shipping** due to quality concerns.
- How do you decide when something is **“good enough” to ship**?

**Depth prompts:**
- What trade-offs did you make between **timeline and quality**?
- How did you **validate** that the experience met your bar?
- How did you advocate for **users or long-term maintainability**?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Taste, quality bar, user focus, technical judgment.

### Cross-Functional Collaboration

**Example prompts:**
- Talk about a time you **worked closely with design/PM/other platforms**.
- Describe a disagreement with a **designer or PM** and how you resolved it.
- Tell me about shaping a feature where **hardware, software, and design** all had constraints.

**Depth prompts:**
- How did you ensure a **shared mental model** across functions?
- Where did you **compromise**, and where did you hold firm?
- How did you ensure the final outcome still **felt cohesive**?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Collaboration, communication, product sensibility.

## Netflix — Culture, Freedom & Responsibility

Netflix interviews often devote a large portion of time to **culture and behavioral questions** anchored in their culture memo (e.g. **Freedom & Responsibility**, **Context not Control**, the **Keeper Test**).

**What interviewers are actively looking for:**
- Comfort operating with **high autonomy** paired with strong accountability.
- **Candor and maturity** in giving and receiving direct feedback.
- A history of making **high-impact, high-judgment decisions**.
- Self-awareness about when you **fit or don’t fit** a culture, and acting accordingly.

### Culture & Values Alignment

**Example prompts:**
- Tell me about a time you **made a big bet with limited oversight**.
- Describe a situation where you **gave or received very direct feedback**.
- If your manager were asked whether they would **fight to keep you**, what would they say and why?

**Depth prompts:**
- How do you balance **freedom with accountability**?
- How do you approach **candid feedback** (both giving and receiving)?
- When have you **chosen to leave or reshaped a team** based on culture fit?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Ownership, maturity, candor, self-awareness.

### Impact & Judgment

**Example prompts:**
- Tell me about the **highest-impact decision** you’ve made in the last few years.
- Describe a time when you **disagreed with leadership** but still had to decide what to do.
- Talk about a time you **intentionally took a risk** that could have failed.

**Depth prompts:**
- How did you evaluate **risk vs. reward**?
- What information did you have, and what did you **explicitly not know**?
- What changed in your approach to **judgment and trade-offs** as a result?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Judgment, impact, strategic thinking.

## How to Practice (General)

Across FAANG-style companies, strong candidates:
- Use **structured answers** (e.g. STAR: Situation, Task, Action, Result).
- Bring **specific, recent stories** that demonstrate ownership, collaboration, and impact.
- Tailor examples to the **company’s language and values** (e.g. Amazon LPs, Googleyness, Meta’s 3 pillars, Netflix culture memo).
- Reflect on what they **learned** and how they **changed their behavior** afterward.
