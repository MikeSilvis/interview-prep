## Prompt

> Hi [NAME], today [other interviewer] and I are here to talk to you about your past experience as an engineer. During this interview, we'll focus on **aspects of the job other than architecture & technical design**, including working with other team members, and how you learn & grow as an engineer.
>
> In this interview, specific examples and situations are key - **tell us about specific projects and specific situations** you've been in, rather than speaking in general terms.
>
> We are going to ask a set of questions, **we might stop you to move on** - that doesn't mean the answer is bad. It means that we've gotten enough signal and want to move on to talk about other topics.
>
> We'll leave 5-10 minutes at the end of the session for any questions you have for us.

---

## Focus Area 1 - Collaboration & Communication (10-20 min)

**Prompt:** Let's start by talking about collaboration & communication.

### Question 1

Let's talk about engineers in other disciplines you've worked with (backend, frontend, other mobile platforms, etc). In particular, tell us about a **complex, cross-functional project** you worked with these engineers on.

**Possible depth prompts:**
- What made the collaboration complex / high-touch?
- Tell me about how you went about resolving communication / understanding / knowledge gaps.
- Tell me about an engineer you especially enjoyed working with in another discipline. What made it go well?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Collaboration, Communication, Team Effectiveness

### Question 2

Next, we'll discuss a **product manager, designer, or other cross-functional partner that you've worked effectively with**. Tell us about a time you **worked with them to plan & scope** a complex, multi-person, or high-impact product feature. How did you determine a timeline?

*(If they haven't worked with product managers or designers, ask about stakeholders or customers who set the product requirements - though you may need to redirect the question a bit to fit their exact situation.)*

**Possible depth prompts:**
- Tell us about a time when you worked with them to balance execution speed with code quality or technical debt. Why was it necessary and what was the outcome?
- Why were you able to work effectively with them?
- What did you learn from them or what did you take away from the relationship?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Collaboration, Communication, Team Effectiveness

---

## Focus Area 2 - Quality & Technical Decision-Making (10-15 min)

**Prompt:** Next, let's talk about how you maintain code quality.

### Question 3

What was **one of the most difficult bugs** you've encountered?

**Possible depth prompts:**
- How did you figure out what fix was needed? What tools and strategies did you use?
- How did you reproduce it?
- *[If they couldn't reproduce it]* How did you determine an appropriate fix?
- What happened afterwards (tech solution, team improvements, setting up for success)?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Awareness, Analysis, Troubleshooting

### Question 4

Talk about a time when you **improved someone's work during a code review**. [If they didn't do code reviews, ask how they maintained the quality of code for the team, including code they didn't write.]

**Possible depth prompts:**
- What are important things you are looking for in code reviews? And can you rank their priority?
- Talk about a time when you were able to improve your own work based on code review feedback you received?
- Inversely, when opening a pull request for review, how do you prep reviewers and provide context?
- (If applicable) How did you communicate your disagreement / how did you teach the other party doing the review?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Awareness, Analysis, Troubleshooting

---

## Focus Area 3 - Learning and Growing (5-10 min)

**Prompt:** Next let's talk about your learning & growth.

### Question 5

Tell me about something important you've **learned in the past year** or two that made you a more effective teammate or engineer. How did you learn it?

**Possible depth prompts:**
- *[If they don't explain why]* Why was it important?
- Did you change your behavior and approach to engineering as a result? How?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Inventing & Learning, Curiosity, Empathy

### Question 6

Could you describe a **major technical mistake** you've made.

**Possible depth prompts:**
- Why was it a mistake? What led to the mistake happening?
- What was the impact of the mistake? Did it cause any further-reaching downsides or problems?
- Did you need to communicate this to anyone else in your organization?
- Did you or your team change anything about how you work as a result?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Inventing & Learning, Curiosity, Empathy, Awareness

---

## Focus Area 4 - Mentorship (5-10 min)

**Prompt:** Finally, let's talk about how you've helped your team members improve their skills.

### Question 7

Tell me how you **helped a co-worker level up** their skills, technically or otherwise.

**Possible depth prompts:**
- How did you see the opportunity to help them?
- How did you go about helping them? Explain each step of your process.
- What was the outcome?
- How did it help their team? Did it impact anyone outside of their team?
- What did you learn from helping them?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Mentorship, Team Effectiveness

### Question 8

Tell me about a **person you've worked directly with that you've learned the most from**. Tell us about them and describe the relationship.

**Possible depth prompts:**
- What did you learn from them?
- How did you incorporate those learnings to improve your work?

**Rubric Dimensions:** Mentorship, Awareness, Curiosity
